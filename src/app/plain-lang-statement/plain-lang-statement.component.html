Plain Language Statement

Who is conducting this research?
This final year research project is being conducted by Ruben Mukherjee and Piyush Vijay Wadhai under the supervision of Prof. Tomas Ward, School of Computing, DCU, and Lili Zhang, School of Computing, DCU.
Title of the research
Conspiracy Theory Belief, Anxiety and Human Decision Making
Research Summary
Research has shown that people with anxiety tend to perform slightly differently than people without anxiety in some psychological tasks. Research has also shown a link between anxiety and tendencies to disbelieve/believe in conspiracy theories. We are combining these two motivations to analyse how anxiety and conspiracy theory-belief influence our decision-making as measured in learning tasks. We will look for patterns in the decision making data collected to determine if there are any differences.
If you agree to participate in this project:
If you decide to take part, you will be directed to the website which contains the surveys and task. First, you will complete a short survey which relates to your belief in conspiracy theories, and then you will be asked to complete another short survey which relates to your experience with anxiety. Finally you will be presented with the decision-making task. In total, you should be able to complete the surveys and task in approximately 25 minutes.
What happens if I change my mind?
Participation in this study is entirely voluntary. You can withdraw your participation from the study for any reason, without penalty, at any point before the submission of your data.
How will my privacy be protected?
Due to the anonymity of the study and the fact that the computer games are completed anonymously, no identifying personal information will be collected. So your privacy and confidentiality will be maintained at all times. We do not ask for your:
  • Name
  • Email
  • Phone No
We do ask for your:
  • Age
  • Gender
